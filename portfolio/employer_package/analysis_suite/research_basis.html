Research basis

What informed the design

A short research map for the strategy choices behind the evidence pages.

This section summarizes the research inputs that informed the design choices, what was carried into the build, and where the implementation deliberately remained conservative.

Working stance: treat the literature as a design input, not as a substitute for validation. The project uses the Nordic momentum research to narrow the search space, then lets Phase 2 validation, holdout testing, and forward observation do the real filtering.

Short version

What the literature changed

  • Main track Pure price momentum in Nordic equities had the clearest support, so it stayed at the center of the implementation.
  • Research track Alpha or residual momentum stayed in the sandbox because its support was thinner and more fragile.
  • Implementation Capacity, crash risk, and cost realism mattered enough that practical constraints were treated as part of the strategy, not as housekeeping.

Key references

The studies that mattered most

Each one below answers the same question: what did it contribute to the actual design choices in this project?

Peer-reviewed article

Grobys, Fatmy & Rajalin (2024)

What it supports: Nordic momentum still appears to exist, and more elaborate combinations do not clearly beat pure momentum after broader risk adjustment.

What we took from it: Keep the main track centred on price momentum instead of adding complexity just to sound more advanced.

What we did not assume: That one paper settles live deployability, costs, or portfolio-construction details for this exact build.

Master's thesis

Nilsson & Picone (2021)

What it supports: Alpha or idiosyncratic momentum may have attractive risk-adjusted properties in Nordic samples.

What we took from it: Alpha deserved its own research lane.

What we did not assume: That it should replace plain price momentum as the default strategy before stronger out-of-sample proof and cost-aware testing.

Master's thesis

Jacobsen & Nyhegn (2021)

What it supports: Momentum in the Nordics appears stronger in smaller companies, which is helpful and risky at the same time.

What we took from it: Capacity and liquidity cannot be an afterthought; they need to shape filters, sizing, and how we talk about the result.

What we did not assume: That a small-cap-heavy academic result should be read as free licence to ignore implementation friction.

Master's thesis

Bergsten (2019)

What it supports: A straightforward price-momentum signal can work, but crash behaviour is real and needs to be measured.

What we took from it: Keep a simple momentum baseline in view and make crash risk a first-class reporting item rather than a footnote.

What we did not assume: That ignoring costs or execution details is acceptable once the backtest looks good.

Master's thesis

Savolainen (2024)

What it supports: Value-plus-momentum combinations can improve the risk profile, but standalone momentum is not uniformly strong in every variant.

What we took from it: Benchmark comparisons and extension tracks matter, but they belong after the baseline has earned trust.

What we did not assume: That the right answer is to add more factors immediately instead of proving the base implementation first.

Process reference

Alpha Architect QMOM case

What it supports: Practical process ideas around screening, rebalancing, and liquidity awareness.

What we took from it: Useful design inspiration for workflow and implementation detail.

What we did not assume: That marketing material counts as independent evidence or Nordic validation.

Interpretation

What we carried into the build

This is the practical through-line from the literature to the implementation choices.

Decision 1

Keep the main track on Nordic price momentum

The literature bundle gave stronger support to plain price momentum than to alpha or residual variants, so the main candidate stayed there.

Decision 2

Treat alpha as a research branch, not the headline strategy

That kept the employer-facing story aligned with the strongest evidence instead of the most exotic idea in the backlog.

Decision 3

Build around realistic constraints

Liquidity, capacity, crash behaviour, and costs shaped the validation stack, not just the discussion section.

Decision 4

Let the validation stack make the final call

The literature narrowed the design space; the validation gates, untouched holdout, and forward-trial plan decided whether the candidate survived.

Bottom line: the project takes the Nordic momentum literature seriously, but translates it into a practical, execution-aware implementation with stricter validation and cost discipline than most academic studies use.
